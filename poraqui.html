PorAqui

Everything around you

Goal

PorAqui is an app that offers hyperlocalized news written by both professional journalists and users.

It fills an information gap by publishing stories that are not big enough for state newspapers but are still relevant for local residents.

My role in this project was to research, design and test a new UI with the goal of improving the user retention and session length metrics.

I was assigned for this 6-week project alongside a team of 2 developers and an advertiser for the Summer Job Program at C.E.S.A.R.


DISCLAIMER: I am not allowed to share any visual work I’ve made for this project.

Therefore, this case study is illustrated by screenshots of older and newer versions of this app, publicly available at Google Play.

Research

Interviews - Current Users

First we interviewed a few passionate and frequent users to understand the value they saw on the product and how we could translate this value into the interface.

Interviews - Potential Users

To avoid a biased result, we also interviewed potential and former users to find out their expectations, why they weren't achieved and how they would use the app.

Creating Personas

Based on this data, we identified 3 different personas with different profiles and demands and created empathy maps to understand their contexts and pains.

Brainstorming Solutions

We used these insights to brainstorm over 15 new features. After presenting these ideas to the client, we prioritized 4 of them to implement in the new experience.

Development

Organizing the Workload

I decided to start the development by making user flows.

Since my teammates didn't have any experience with UX/UI Design, this helped us grasp the amount of work we needed to do, what the complete experience would be like and how to invest our time and skills in the most efficient way.

I also created a post-it board based on the Scrum methodology to manage the tasks we needed to do.

Designing the Wireframes

Once the structure was defined, we started thinking of different solutions for each screen, using similar apps and reference boards as an inspiration.

At first, we used pen and paper to make sure we could iterate quickly without worrying about our own biases and critics.

Then, we selected a few options and built higher fidelity wireframes using Adobe XD. This assured the screens had a cleaner look when presenting and testing our prototype.

Prototyping and Testing

With the screens ready, we used inVision to built the prototype used for user testing.

Since we already had mapped our user flow it was easy to quickly switch between different options on our prototype, what made the user testing more time-efficient.

The tests resulted in an overall positive feedback, so we moved on to the delivery.

Delivery

Narrated Prototype

We chose to present as our main deliverable a narrated prototype, in additional to wireframes and written documentation.

Since the features would be implemented by another team at a later date, the video showing all the interactions step-by-step helps other developers and designers understand our intentions clearly even when we’re not there.

Client Feedback

Our client worked with us from the beginning of this project and we had weekly meetings to showcase our work and align expectations.

At the end, our client was satisfied with our work and today I can see that many of the features we designed are already implemented in the app.

Learnings

This project had many challenges, but the most difficult was to instruct the team on how to do an entire UX process in 6 weeks.

My coworkers didn’t have any prior experience with UX Design, so I was acting as a designer and team leader at the same time.

Thankfully we were able to understand early on what each member skills and interests were and we distributed the tasks accordingly, making sure everyone would enjoy and be proud of their participation on the project.
