HelpNet

Accountability for recovering addicts

Opportunity

According to the National Institute of Drug Abuse, 40 to 60% of patients who undergo drug addiction treatment relapse.

This project aims to decrease this number by detecting risk behaviour and immediatly connecting the patient with their support network.

User Journey

I chose as the main public for this app the patients who are finishing treatment and getting out of a rehab facility.

Since they are experiencing a difficult transition period, they are the ones who an app like this one would help the most.

When a user starts the recovery process, the first recommendation is to avoid people and places related to the addiction.

However, it's very hard to do this, especially in a moment of crisis when the pacient is feeling alone and more prone to relapse.

A typical relapse journey looks like this:

1. The user finishes treatment and gets out of the facility

2. They distance themselves from the program

3. Back to their original environment, stress starts piling up

4. The user goes back to their old habits, starting with small things

5. The user isolates themselves from their friends and sponsor

6. Feeling hurt and lonely, the user loses control and relapses

Solution

During the research process, I realized that what most commonly drives people to relapse is feeling lonely and returning to old habits.

With this in mind, I created an app that detects when the user contacts people that could drive them to relapse, blocks any calls and texts and immediately connects the user with their support network, providing enough time to recover and strength to resist this urge.

The onboarding experience was designed to make the user feel comfortable and confident. It consist of 4 steps:


Name

This makes the experience more personal and intimate.

Unsafe Contacts

People who the user needs to keep contact with, but can lead to dangerous situations, like some coworkers.

Interface

After the setup, the user can see 3 tabs:

Blocked

This tab is blocked by default to prevent the user from calling the contacts from another phone.

Friends

People on this list will be notified by text if the user reachs out to blocked or unsafe contacts and can get the code to unlock and edit blocked contacts after the first setup.

Attention

The list of unsafe contacts. They will not be blocked but the app will send a text to everyone in the support group.


Learnings

Expanded Public

Despite being designed for recovering addicts, this app is helpful for anyone who needs to avoid harmful contexts and addict behaviour, no matter if it's related to drugs, food or an abusive partner. This realizations is reflected on the somewhat generic interface.

Platform Differences

During this project, I learned a lot about the process of building apps and the differences between designing for Android and IOS, especially when it comes to what is allowed or not on each platform.

Technical Limitations

I know many of the features designed need permissions that are not possible to get. Still, I enjoyed working on a product that I'm confident could help people, even if just as an inspiration.
