HelpNet

Accountability for recovering addicts

Opportunity

According to the National Institute of Drug Abuse, 40 to 60% of patients who undergo drug addiction treatment relapse.

This project aims to decrease this number by detecting risk behaviour and immediatly connecting the patient with their support network.

Research

Before working on solutions, I wanted to understand the context of drug abuse and addiction treatments.

Due to time constrains and the sensitivity of this theme, instead of basing my research on the direct input of potential users, I worked with a family member who’s a specialist in alcohol and drugs addiction.

Questions I explored:

In what moment / context would this app be most useful?

What makes patients relapse? What keeps some of them from relapsing?

Who else would use this solution?

User Journey

I chose as the main public for this app the patients who are finishing treatment and getting out of a rehab facility. Since they are experiencing a difficult transition period, they are the ones who an app like this one would help the most.

When a user starts the recovery process, the first recommendation is to avoid people and places related to the addiction. However, it's very hard to do this, especially in a moment of crisis when the pacient is feeling alone and more prone to relapse.

After this realization, I decided to develop a solution focused on this moment of crisis and solitute, using 3 scenarios to consider a few reasons why the crisis begins in the first place.

Scenario 1

Louise (28, F) has been clean for 1 year, but she has just lost her job. She feels depressed and is at risk of relapsing.

- She tries to talk to her therapist, but can’t reach him.

- It’s late and Louise doesn’t want to bother her loved ones with her problems, so she doesn’t call them.

- Louise feels lonely, but she remembers of an old friend who wouldn’t mind if she wants to go out for just one drink.

Scenario 2

It’s Shaun’s (16, M) 3rd week out of rehab. He’s doing great, but he’s starting to feel lonely.

- One of Shaun’s old friends sends a text asking to meet up.

- Shaun misses his friends, so he texts his old girlfriend. She asks him to join her at the skate park they used to go.

- Shaun knows a lot of people go there to use drugs, but he doesn’t think it will be a problem for him.

Scenario 3

Paul (40, M) has been home for 3 months. He’s doing everything he’s supposed to, but his family won’t trust him.

- His daughter refuses to go out, claiming he’s just using her.

- He’s having dinner with his wife. She won’t talk to him. She’s still hurt from his previous abusive behaviour.

- Paul passes athrough bar getting out of from work. He’s scared of going home, so he gets in to avoid it.

Delivery

Narrated Prototype

We chose to present as our main deliverable a narrated prototype, in additional to wireframes and written documentation.

Since the features would be implemented by another team at a later date, the video showing all the interactions step-by-step helps other developers and designers understand our intentions clearly even when we’re not there.

Client Feedback

Our client worked with us from the beginning of this project and we had weekly meetings to showcase our work and align expectations.

At the end, our client was satisfied with our work and today I can see that many of the features we designed are already implemented in the app.

Learnings

This project had many challenges, but the most difficult was to instruct the team on how to do an entire UX process in 6 weeks.

My coworkers didn’t have any prior experience with UX Design, so I was acting as a designer and team leader at the same time.

Thankfully we were able to understand early on what each member skills and interests were and we distributed the tasks accordingly, making sure everyone would enjoy and be proud of their participation on the project.
